#02

De Diego, a Irina.

                The sun will set and my thoughts of you will be revealed, like all the stars in the sky. Forming constellations of desires. And there I will be, inside this illusion where I imagine that being together someday is possible. Lost in a future that only exists in my mind.

I will hold these thoughts until the night has faded. When the dawn breaks with its light and reminds me that I must carry on with life, with the routine I have led until now. Can you take from someone something you have never had? Why does it feel as if the loss of this dream hurts more than any reality?

Tell me that you love me, clear my doubts like the wind dissipates the morning mist. Make your voice the anchor that prevents me from shouting: "Where are you?". Do not leave me alone in this darkness, if I have already believed every word you said. Even the unspoken ones that resonated in the silence between us.

See? The moon cannot warm this place, this cold room in this part of my city. I miss you in every moment, in every breath I take, I look for you in places whose nostalgia would break a lonely heart. Do you look for me in the fields, where the grass is green and wide, stretching forever along the dusty road?

Do you find me in the colors where the auroras boreales wander, or on a lonely park bench on your quiet walk home? Or am I just a ghost haunting the edges of your life, crossing through the door you left open, with the delicacy of a warm spring breeze soaked in the scent of mixed flowers? I will think: Just in time! If you had closed it a minute later, none of this would have happened and I would be setting my alarm to wake up early tomorrow.

I will also tell you that I love you and that I have come to kiss you as if it were the first time in life. And the only time that really matters. So that you look into my eyes and let "I love you" be the words that come from your lips. To be held the way people hold each other in a farewell, until I feel my own bones turn to dust. Broken by the weight of a desperate embrace.

I would take off your scarf like a child opening a gift, just to feel the softness of your neck before our spirits rise. Are you perhaps so warm, that even the winter sun would want to be in your arms? Before the cruel hand of the morning snatches you from my reach. Like waking from a dream that I cannot hold onto. I wake up alone, in a bed for one; alone and the only warmth I feel is that of the tears sliding down my cheeks.
